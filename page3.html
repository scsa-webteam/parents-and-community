How is my child going?

Schools provide formal reports on a student's learning progress at key times throughout the year.

Most students will receive regular feedback throughout the year. This should mean that the information presented in a report does not come as a surprise to students or their parents.

Feedback

Feedback on a child's progress may be formal or informal.

Feedback about a student's achievement and learning progress should be ongoing. It should reflect the needs of the student, the family and the school community.

Feedback may include verbal comments to students and/or to parents, written comments on work, letters of concern or congratulations, merit certificates, or interviews.

Reporting

What are the rules for reporting on my child’s progress in K–10?+

The School Curriculum and Standards Authority requires all schools to implement the Western Australian Curriculum and Assessment Outline to meet the learning needs of all students. The rules for reporting are set out in the Authority’s Pre-Primary to Year 10: Teaching, Assessing and Reporting Policy sets out the mandatory requirements for teachers to implement the Outline.

The policy is supported by Policy Standards for Pre-primary to Year 10: Teaching, Assessing and Reporting. The Standards provide the minimum requirements for curriculum planning and reporting on student achievement.

The Teaching, Assessing and Reporting Policy includes procedures for schools in relation to: curriculum, what teachers need to teach; assessment, how teachers need to assess student learning; and reporting, how teachers need to report on student achievement.

Schools are expected to provide their school community with an assessment and reporting policy that is based on the principles of learning, teaching and assessment.

The Pre-primary to Year 10: Teaching, Assessing and Reporting Policy and the Standards are available on our website.

What should a report feature in K–10?+

The Authority expects schools to use plain language to report to parents and carers. Reports on the achievements of Pre-primary to Year 10 students are expected to be in terms of the Western Australian achievement standards.

When should I receive reports on my child’s progress in K–10?+

A formal report will be provided in a formal report at the end of each semester. Additional informal reports may be provided. Parents and carers may also request interim reports on children’s progress.

Schools are also expected to share reports from national and statewide assessments with parents and carers. Where appropriate, schools should provide opportunities for discussion between teachers and parents or carers about reports from national and statewide assessments.

What should reports for my K–10 child include?+

The end-of-semester report should be in plain English. It is expected to be accurate and objective about a student’s progress and achievement in relation to the Western Australian achievement standards detailed in the Outline. A five-point scale/achievement descriptor is required.

  • In the Pre-primary year, student achievement is reported using achievement descriptors and without letter grades.
  • For Years 1 to 2, student achievement is reported using system-based or school-based achievement descriptors or those provided in the Standards. Schools may use letter grades.
  • For Years 3 to 10, letter grades and achievement descriptors as outlined in the Standards must be used.

All schools must implement the learning area reporting requirements outlined in the standards. Schools may choose to report at a more detailed level than the minimum requirements identified. School systems may direct schools to report at a level above the minimum requirements.

In mid-year reports, teachers make a professional judgment regarding the level of achievement that the student is demonstrating relative to the achievement standard, taking into account the curriculum that has been taught and assessed to that point in time.

My K–10 child is following a modified curriculum, how will that affect reporting?+

If there is a legitimate reason for a student to be following a modified curriculum schools report on a student’s progress/achievement in terms of the modified curriculum. Additional reporting on the student’s progress/achievement in terms of the year-level achievement standard is not required by the Authority.

What should reports include when my child is in Year 11 or 12?+

In Years 11 and 12, student achievement in the form of marks and grades is reported to the student, their parents/guardians/carers and to the Authority.

The commonly accepted practice is that the school reports the school mark and grade for each pair of Year 11 and 12 pair of units completed (i.e. one mark and one grade) or for each Year 11 unit completed (i.e. a mark and grade for Unit 1 and a mark and grade for Unit 2). Typically this is provided in the school’s Semester 2 report and/or in a school‐developed statement of results.

Students, and their parents/guardians/carers, should be aware that:

  • the grades reported by the school are not finalised until approved by the Authority
  • the school marks for ATAR Year 12 pairs of units are statistically adjusted (moderated) by the Authority. These adjustments reflect the ATAR course examination performance of students in a school that has Year 12 ATAR school marks on a scale that is significantly different from the scale of the ATAR course examination marks of those students.
